Action Steps for 30-Somethings

Max out 401(k) match first: If your employer matches 6%, contribute at least 6%. That's free money you can't get anywhere else.
Then fill your Roth IRA: $7,000/year. If married, that's $14,000 combined. Do this before anything else after the 401(k) match.
House vs. investing decision: Don't let a house down payment delay investing. Do both. Save for house in HYSA, invest in retirement accounts simultaneously.
Have the money talk with your partner: You need to be aligned on investing. Schedule monthly "money dates" to review progress together.
Increase with every raise: Got a 5% raise? Invest 3%, lifestyle upgrade 2%. This is how you scale without feeling the pinch.
Teach your kids early: Open custodial accounts. Show them your portfolio. Normalize talking about money and investing.

Common Mistakes in Your 30s

  • Lifestyle inflation: Nicer house, nicer car, nicer vacations. Every upgrade delays your millionaire status by years.
  • Keeping up with the Joneses: Your neighbors are probably broke. Don't compete with people going into debt.
  • Pausing for kids: "We'll invest more after daycare" never comes. Invest during the chaos, not after.
  • Buying too much house: Just because you're approved for $500K doesn't mean you should spend it. Stay under budget, invest the difference.
  • Cashing out 401(k) when changing jobs: Roll it over! Never cash out retirement accounts. The taxes and penalties are devastating.

The Partner Conversation

If you're married or in a serious relationship, you MUST be aligned on money. Here's how to handle it:

Monthly money date: Same day every month. Review accounts, celebrate milestones, adjust as needed. Make it fun, not stressful.
Shared goals: "Our goal is $1M by 60" is more motivating than "I want to invest more." Make it about the team.
Automate to avoid arguments: Money goes automatically to investments. You can't fight about what happens on autopilot.
Individual fun money: After investments, each person gets guilt-free spending money. No questions asked. This prevents resentment.
