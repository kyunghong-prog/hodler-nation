Maximum Impact Strategies for Your 50s

Use catch-up contributions: Age 50+ gets extra: $7,500 for 401(k), $1,000 for IRA. That's $31,000/year tax-advantaged.
Consider Roth conversions: If in lower tax bracket now, convert traditional IRA to Roth. Pay taxes now, grow tax-free.
Delay Social Security to 70: Every year you wait from 67-70 increases benefit by 8%. That's a 24% boost for waiting 3 years.
Health Savings Account (HSA): Triple tax advantage. Max it out ($4,150 individual, $8,300 family in 2025). Use it as retirement account.
Eliminate ALL debt before 65: Mortgage, car loans, credit cards - everything. Entering retirement debt-free changes everything.
Practice retirement budget NOW: Live on your projected retirement income for 6 months. See if it's realistic. Adjust goals accordingly.

The Social Security Strategy

This is critically important in your 50s:

Claiming at 62: You can start early but benefits reduced by 30%. Only do this if you absolutely need money.
Claiming at 67 (Full Retirement Age): You get 100% of your benefit. This is the baseline.
Claiming at 70 (Maximum): You get 124% of your full benefit. If you can wait, this dramatically increases lifetime payout.
Spousal strategies: Lower-earning spouse can claim spousal benefits. Coordinate timing for maximum household benefit.

Healthcare Bridge Strategy

If you retire before 65 (Medicare eligibility), healthcare is your biggest challenge:

COBRA: Continue employer insurance for 18 months. Expensive but comprehensive.
ACA Marketplace: Shop healthcare.gov. Subsidies available based on income. Can be affordable if you manage income.
Part-time work with benefits: Starbucks, UPS, Costco offer health insurance to part-timers. Seriously consider this.
Plan for $1,000-2,000/month: Budget this for healthcare if retiring before 65. It's the biggest early retirement expense.
