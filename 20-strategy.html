Action Steps for 20-Somethings

Open a Roth IRA immediately: Contributions grow tax-free forever. Max it out if you can ($7,000/year in 2025). Your future self will thank you.
Automate everything: Set up automatic monthly investments. Make it so you never have to think about it. The money comes out before you can spend it.
Live below your means: Avoid lifestyle inflation. Just because you got a raise doesn't mean you need a nicer apartment. Invest the difference.
Side hustle your way up: Extra $500/month from freelancing? That's $600K+ by retirement. Your 20s are perfect for hustling.
Employer 401(k) match = free money: Always get the full match. That's an instant 50-100% return on your money. Nothing beats that.
Learn to hold through crashes: You'll see 3-5 major crashes before 65. Every one is a buying opportunity, not a reason to sell.

Common Mistakes to Avoid in Your 20s

  • Waiting to start: "I'll invest when I make more money" is the biggest wealth killer. Start with $50/month if that's all you have.
  • Timing the market: You can't. No one can. Just buy and hold.
  • Following crypto bros: SPY and QQQ are boring. That's the point. Boring makes you rich.
  • Cashing out for "emergencies": Build a separate emergency fund. Never touch your investments.
  • Buying individual stocks: You're not smarter than the market. Stick to index funds.
  • Lifestyle inflation: Every raise should go 50% to investments, not a nicer car.

Real Talk: Can You Actually Do This?

Investing $400/month feels impossible? Here's how 20-somethings actually do it:

The side hustle: Freelancing, DoorDash on weekends, selling stuff online. Extra $500/month is very doable.
The roommate route: Living with roommates instead of alone saves $500-800/month in most cities.
The car decision: Cheap used car vs. new = $400+/month saved.
The subscription audit: Cancel everything you don't use daily. Gym, streaming services, subscriptions add up to $100-200/month.
The lunch strategy: Meal prep = $300/month saved vs eating out.
